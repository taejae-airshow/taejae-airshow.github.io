What is AIR?
AIR — Advanced Independent Research — is a highly selective program for students pursuing self-directed projects beyond regular coursework. It is designed for those aiming to produce a graduate-school writing sample, undertake substantial faculty-collaborative research, or build a product portfolio for jobs and internships. Only students who bring seriousness, persistence, and creativity are selected.
Who can participate?
AIR is open to Taejae students ready to take full ownership of a significant project. Work may be individual or team-based, and interdisciplinary collaborations across programs are encouraged. Faculty supervision is matched to each project's primary domain.
How does voting work?
During an active semester, any Taejae community member can browse the Gallery and cast a vote for projects they find most compelling.
What are the awards?
Each semester, the faculty committee presents two awards — the Innovative Research Award for research originality and the Real-world Impact Award for potential to create meaningful change. The Outstanding Presentation Award is voted on by the entire Taejae community. All winners are enshrined in the Hall of Fame.
